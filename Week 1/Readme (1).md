# Week 1

Week 1 focuses on the foundational ideas of computer architecture. These lectures revisit core concepts from an introductory architecture course and build the vocabulary needed for the rest of the course.

If some of these ideas feel unfamiliar or difficult, that is completely okay. Take time to look up additional resources, ask questions, and revisit the material carefully. These are important concepts, and they will be used repeatedly in later weeks.

## Topics Covered

### Lecture 1
An introduction to the history of computing, this is a bit theoretical. You can explore different youtube videos which cover the timeline in a much more visual form. This should encourage you to think about how far we have come, starting from room sized computers to hand held beasts.

### Lecture 2
The stored-program model, the fetch-decode-execute cycle, and the motivation for pipelining.

### Lecture 3
Instruction Set Architecture (ISA), the hardware-software contract, RISC vs. CISC, and the memory wall.

### Lecture 4
Cache organization, cache performance, and the basics of out-of-order execution.

## Weekly Assignment

The Week 1 assignment is designed to help you think about ISA design, instruction encoding, and tradeoffs in a constrained architecture. The goal is not just to get an answer, but to understand why different design choices matter.

## What You Should Take Away

By the end of Week 1, you should be comfortable with:

- the stored-program concept
- the fetch-decode-execute cycle
- the basics of pipelining
- what an ISA is and why it matters
- the difference between RISC and CISC
- why caches exist
- the idea of out-of-order execution

This week is meant to build a strong base for the more advanced topics in the rest of the course.
