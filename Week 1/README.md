# Welcome to the Course!

Welcome to this 4-week course on computer architecture.

Over the next few weeks, we will explore some of the most interesting ideas shaping the modern compute industry. The goal of this course is not to overwhelm you with technical detail, but to spark curiosity and encourage you to explore new ideas on your own.

By the end of the course, you should be familiar with several important terms, concepts, and design ideas that will help you understand industry discussions, technical conversations, and the latest developments in computing.

This course will require steady effort. You are expected to go through the weekly content and complete the weekly assignment. The assignments are designed to be thought-provoking rather than purely theoretical.

## Course Structure

- **Week 1:** Foundational concepts from a first course in computer architecture
- **Week 2 onward:** New topics beyond the standard introductory material

Week 1 is a review of a few core concepts from a first course in computer architecture. If these ideas feel difficult at first, please take time to explore additional resources online. These concepts will be essential for everything that follows.

From Week 2 onward, we will move beyond the standard material and explore newer ideas and more advanced topics in computer architecture.

## What to Expect

This course is designed to help you:

- build intuition for how modern computers work
- understand the vocabulary used in the computing industry
- connect foundational concepts to current hardware trends
- think critically about design tradeoffs in computer systems

We hope you enjoy the course and find it both useful and exciting.
